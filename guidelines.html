The House Rules

Community Guidelines.

Effective Feb. 1, 2026 · Last Updated Feb. 1, 2026

WildHeavy is built by eaters, for eaters. These guidelines help us maintain a community where authentic dining experiences are shared, trusted voices are heard, and everyone can discover what to order next.

01 · Core Principles.

1.1 Eat It to Rank It

Every ranking you submit should reflect a dish you've actually eaten. WildHeavy's value comes from authentic, first-hand experiences. Speculation, hearsay, or rankings based on reputation alone undermine the community.

1.2 Comped Meal Disclosure

If your meal was comped by the restaurant — invited, gifted, or otherwise provided at no cost — you can still rate it fairly. Just be transparent. A comped meal is sponsored content, and the community deserves to know. When you build the Menu Card, mark the meal as comped and a disclosure note will appear on the card.

The Rule: Comped meals can be rated honestly. Disclosure builds trust; hiding it erodes it.

1.3 Conflict of Interest

If you own, manage, or work for a restaurant, you may rate your own establishment honestly. However, you must not:

  • Use your account to negatively target competitors
  • Create multiple accounts to inflate your restaurant's rankings
  • Coordinate with others to manipulate scores

1.4 Respect the House

When sharing dining experiences, be respectful of restaurant staff, fellow diners, and the establishments themselves:

  • Do not share private information about staff members (doxxing)
  • Do not encourage illegal activity, trespassing, or health code violations
  • Critique the food, not the people

02 · Prohibited Content.

The following content is strictly prohibited and will result in removal and potential account suspension:

  • Hate Speech & Discrimination. Content that promotes hatred, violence, or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.
  • Violence & Threats. Threats of violence, encouragement of harm, or content that glorifies violent acts against individuals, groups, or establishments.
  • Spam & Manipulation. Repetitive posting, bot-generated content, coordinated inauthentic behavior, or any attempt to artificially inflate engagement or rankings.
  • Private Information. Sharing personal information about others without consent, including home addresses, phone numbers, financial information, or identity documents.

03 · Ranking Integrity.

WildHeavy's rankings are designed to surface the best dishes based on real community input. Here's how we maintain integrity.

3.1 What We Do

  • Weight trusted voices. Rankings from users with demonstrated expertise (Industry Pros, Tastemakers) carry more influence in our scoring algorithm.
  • Apply freshness signals. Recent experiences are weighted more heavily than older ones to reflect current menu quality.
  • Deduplicate votes. Each user's most recent ranking for a city + category combination counts. You can update your rankings anytime.
  • Exclude deleted accounts. When an account is deleted, its rankings are removed from active leaderboard calculations.

3.2 What We Don't Do

  • No paid placements. Restaurants cannot pay to appear higher in our rankings. Period.
  • No score purchases. There is no way for a restaurant to buy a better position. Rankings are earned through community votes.
Why we don't reveal exact formulas: Sharing precise algorithmic details would make gaming easier. What matters is that our system rewards authentic, consistent, and trusted voices, not volume or manipulation.

04 · The "Janitor" & Data Hygiene.

WildHeavy uses AI-assisted tools we call "The Janitor" to maintain clean, consistent data across the platform.

What The Janitor Does

  • Normalizes dish names for consistency (e.g., "Cheeseburger" vs "cheese burger")
  • Matches dishes to canonical menu items across restaurants
  • Flags ambiguous entries for review
Our Promise: We fix the data, not your opinion. The Janitor standardizes spelling and naming. It never changes your ratings, notes, or the substance of your reviews.

05 · Reporting & Enforcement.

5.1 Report Violations

If you encounter content that violates these guidelines, use the in-app "Report" button available on rankings, Menu Cards, and user profiles. All reports are reviewed by our team.

5.2 Block Users

You can block any user to prevent them from viewing your content, following you, or appearing in your feed. Manage blocked users in your account settings.

5.3 Review Timeline

We aim to review all reports within 48 hours. Complex cases may take longer. You'll be notified of outcomes when applicable.

5.4 Enforcement Actions

Depending on the severity and frequency of violations, we may take the following actions:

  • Content removal
  • Warning notification
  • Temporary account suspension
  • Permanent account termination

06 · Questions?

If you have questions about these guidelines or need to report an issue, contact us at support@wildheavy.com.
